How tidas works

Apple's new APIs allows Touch ID to unlock iOS's Secure Enclave so it can digitally sign session token for authentication. Authentication only happens when the user touches the iPhone's button. Once Tida's installed in your app:

1

The user opens your app and presents her fingerprint

2

Touch ID confirms the users' fingerprint and converts it into a unique singature

3

Operating in the Secure Enclave, Tidas converts Touch ID's signature into a new crypto key

4

The crypto key passes from your app to your website, and finally to us

5

When we authenticate the signature, we return an 'OK' to your website and app

6

The user enters your app in seconds

The public keys and signatures exposed to your application and to Tidas are useless without the private key stored safely and permanently inside the Secure Enclave. Because jailbreakers can't acess the Secure Enclave, they can't steal the private keys.

In the first login, the user will have to authenticate with a username and password. In subsequent logins, she'll securely and uniquely re-identify with her fingerprint.

Hosting

Tidas is hosted as a service. Middleware is the go-between users and Tidas. Tidas maintains the valueless database of public keys to confirm successful user authentications.

Absolute privacy guaranteed

If the Tidas database were breached tomorrow, the attackers would gain nothing.

That's because Tidas doesn't store any data off of users' devices. (Start a free trial to see our source code). User's tuples stay in the Secure Enclave. Even if an attacker saw the entire Tidas database, he couldn't correlate groups of login tuples to users. Tidas just provides an authenticated/not authenticated decision.

Tidas doesn't collect fingerprints. That's Touch ID's job. It collexts users' fingerprints for authentication and stores them in Secure Enclave. That's completely opaque to Tidas. By design, Tidas never knows who's logging into your app, and you never have to touch users' login credentials.
